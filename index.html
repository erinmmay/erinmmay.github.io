      Welcome! I'm Erin M. May!      

    Postdoctoral researcher at JHU Applied Physics Lab

    I'm broadly interested in the characterization of exoplanet atmospheres. I work with ground and space based telescopes to determine the compositions of their atmospheres, and use 3D climate models to better understand the various physics that affect what we observe.

    I did my Ph.D. in Astronomy & Astrophysics at the University of Michigan in Prof. Emily Rauscher's research group. 
    Before that I got my B.S. degrees in Astrophysics and Mathematics from Michigan State University.
